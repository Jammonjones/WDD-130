Making your words work for you

It is definitely not easy using your words to create the world that you would like, but there are a few simple guidelines to help you to become the person that uses your words wisely to create the results that you want instead of letting your words destroy your hopes and dreams by breaking down the very thing that you are trying to build. Step 1 - Become Aware Start paying attention to what you tell yourself each day. This can be hard so the best way to start is by paying attention to your thoughts when you are feeling down in the day and start linking your thoughts with your performance and how you are feeling. Tip: Start taking 10 minutes everyday and doing mindfulness meditation. This is where you stop doing things and pay attention to what is going on all around you and also inside yourself. This way you can start paying attention to your thoughts and gain more control of what is usually going on unconsciously inside yourself. Step 2 - Alter The Instructions that don’t serve you Think of something realistic that you could believe yourself about how you are going to perform. It is essentially that you can believe it yourself or else your brain will spit it out as bad instructions and not take them seriously. Step 3 - Give Yourself New Instructions Tell yourself your new positive instructions that help you to achieve your goals a few times.

Extra Tips

For becoming aware: Start taking note of the negative instructions that you give yourself each day that aren’t helping you achieve your goals. For changing your instructions: Take the list of things that you have told yourself that have not been helping you achieve your goals and create a new list of things to tell yourself that are in line with attaining your goals. Again make sure to only write down things that are believable and realistic or else your mind will not accept them as valid instructions. For making your words work for you: In order for you to make any significant changes in your life you are going to need to implement a system for change. I suggest setting aside 10 minutes every day for a mindfulness meditation and another 10 minutes to read through the new list of instructions that you have created for yourself so that your new instructions really get ingrained in your brain.
